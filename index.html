PyritePlate or, that shit I keep doing

The boilerplate CSS I normally write and rewrite on my projects, now all together in one shiny spot. Yeeey.

If you somehow got here & wanna grab code, bear in mind it's not my cleanest stuff - not fully responsive, lazily done... it's the sort of shit I use on personal projects just for fun.

Containers

There's a .container that's wrapping all this shit.

<div class="container">
  Your content here
</div>

avoiding stuff is my specialty, though. you can do it too:

<div class="avoid-container">
  all the problems that you're avoiding, here!
</div>

Buttons

For buttons, just use the standard html tag. Add .outline, .primary or .secondary for extra flair. It will be a tad lighter if it's disabled.

.sm buttons will be smaller, and thus, cuter.

Links

URLs like this one will style themselves to be cute.

Links with the .button class will be styled as buttons, and will react to the standard button helper classes.

Not a button would've fool'd ya tho

Forms

I don't normally do forms so there's very few shit imma do here.

<div class="control">
  <label>Name</label>
  <input placeholder="Enter your whatever">
</div>

Add the .error class to style shit when users mess up, and use a .message to display any error texts.

Something's awfully wrong! >:c

Radio button and checkboxes don't need anything fancy, just add a label tag to them.

Grids, ugh

The standard 12 column magic, by the grace of a .grid parent .md# for each cell. Breaks to .sm# at 1024px, and to .lg# at 2048px.

Use .start#, .sm-start# and .lg-start# to offset your cells, indicating on which column should your cell start.

.md2.lg12
.md4
.md1
.md1.start9
.md3.sm6

<div class="grid example">
  <div class="md2 lg12">.md2.lg12</div>
  <div class="md4">.md4</div>
  <div class="md1">.md1</div>
  <div class="md1 start9">.md1.start9</div>
  <div class="md3 sm6">.md3.sm6</div>
</div>

Separator

Sometimes, your content can be really different from other content. Sometimes you wanna split it in sections! Just use the hr HTML tag and you're all set, right?

That's ok, that's why i tweaked this shit! The data-text attribute will allow you to add some text to it, too.

Breathe in, breathe out, kick someone's ass. Everything's gonna be just ggggggreat.

This supports the standard colors

<hr class="secondary" data-text="both of them"/>
